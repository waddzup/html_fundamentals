This web page is a few thoughts about the use of images on the BBC website

The BBC website has very different styles accross its' pages. The main news pages tend to use blocks for each news item or interest item. The block will contain a short piece of text, an image, and a category for the item. The image may be a stock image e.g for an article on alcohol it may just show two glasses, for a football item it may be the actual footballer concerned or the shirt of the football club. In all cases the image can be considered decorative as the text describes the article and acts as a link.

Parts of the BBC website have greater branding and a stronger visual style such as the childrens education section Bitsize, the Horizon page and the Proms pages. These use distinctive background images for headers, footers and section breaks.

The page Earth - filming techniques has a background image at the top of the page that is underwater video footage. The main Earth website has a moonlit beach background image that moves slightly as you move your mouse across the page

One section of the BBC that will always use informative imagery is the weather page which always has a UK map with symbols indicating the kind of weather to be expected. The weather map uses a background of the UK

I haven't figured out how the weather symbols get superimposed. Hopefully by the end of the course I will.
